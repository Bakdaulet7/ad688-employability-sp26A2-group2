About this analysis

This site is a class project for Boston University's MS in Applied Business Analytics, exploring how generative AI is reshaping the US labor market through the lens of posted salaries.

▸ The motivation

Why this question matters

Every quarter brings new headlines about AI either creating six-figure jobs or wiping them out. Both stories can be true at the same time — and they are. We wanted to see, in actual posted-salary data, where the AI premium is real, where it's small, and where it inverts.

The three views on the home page are designed to be read together. The skill chart shows the supply-side story (which technical skills employers will pay for). The experience chart shows the career-stage story (where in your career the AI label adds the most value). The state chart shows the geography story (where that premium concentrates).

What we found, in one sentence

The AI premium is real but uneven — it's biggest for entry-level workers learning to build AI systems in cloud-heavy tech states, and it shrinks toward zero for senior workers, traditional analyst tools, and regions where AI hiring leans toward analyst roles rather than ML engineering.
